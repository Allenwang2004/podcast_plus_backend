3 Joint PMF We can fully describe two discrete RVs X and Y by the joint PMF. Suppose two discrete RVs X and Y are defined over the same sample space, the joint PMF pX,Y(x,y) is defined by pX,Y(x,y) = P(X=x and Y=y) = P( {ω: X(ω) = x and Y(ω) = y} ) From the additivity and normalization axioms of probability, we must have Σx,y pX,Y(x,y) = 1 Example: Rolling two 4-sided die. Let X be the outcome of the first roll and Y of the second, then pX,Y(x,y) = 1/16; for x=1,2,3,4; y=1,2,3,4
