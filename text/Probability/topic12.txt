15 Problem formulation: RV Θ takes one of m values, θ1, …, θm. Once the measurement RV X is observed (with value x), we’d like to decide which hypothesis (one out of θ1, …, θm) is true.  MAP detection rule: Pick up θi based on the maximum pΘ|X(θi |x).  The case of m =2 is called the MAP binary hypothesis test (null and alternate) H0: Θ = θ1 H1: Θ = θ2 Tie: If there is a tie, either can be selected arbitrarily. MAP detection is the decision rule that minimizes the probability of incorrect decision More on MAP Hypothesis Testing
