23 Superposition Principle 0 If two or more traveling waves are moving through a medium, the resultant value of the wave function at any point is the algebraic sum of the values of the wave functions of the individual waves. 0 Waves that obey the superposition principle are linear waves. For mechanical waves, linear waves usually have amplitudes much smaller than their wavelengths. Waves that violate the superposition principle are called non-linear waves, which usually have larger amplitudes. 45  Interference 0 The combination of separate waves in the same region of space to produce a resultant wave is called interference. 46
